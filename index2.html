Allegation count with respect to types and years.

This is a stacked area chart. It shows the breakdown of a category for each year, from 2014 to 2018, with a thicker band indicating that a finding or allegation makes up a higher percentage of a year's total. The data here is by-allegation, meaning that the data visible are the allegations for each complaint filed. Each allegation in a complaint has its own finding.





Allegations with respect to ethnicity.

This is a Sankey chart that shows how many allegations were made against officers of each race from complainants of each race. A tall rectangle indicates a higher volume of allegations made against officers of that race or allegations made by a higher volume of complainants of that race. Coming out of each rectangle are several bands, which together total all allegations made against officers of that race. A thicker band indicates a higher volume. For example, White officers had by far the most allegations, likely because they make up most of the police force. Black complainants filed among the most allegations against white officers, with only a small portion of black complainants filing allegations toward black officers, likely because they make up a smaller part of the force. This chart does not account for the demographical makeup as the chart above, this is a raw representation of every allegation made against the department.





Allgeations against Current NYPD officers.

This map is mainly for the users to get information about the total number of allegations or the count of allegations against the different ethnicities of NYPD officers who are cuurently working in the department of New York city.
